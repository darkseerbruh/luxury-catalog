# Authentication-Markers Brief — Journal series "{House} authentication: the markers worth checking"

*Archivist deliverable, built 2026-06-30. Feeds the Journal authentication-guide series and
the copywriter. This is research, not finished copy — run `brand-voice` before publishing
anything reader-facing.*

## Binding frame for every guide (the calibrated hedge)

**Authentication is markers to check, not a verdict.** Every guide states this up top and
again at the foot. We give buyers the things a real piece does, era by era, and the
inconsistencies that warrant scrutiny. We never claim a single marker "proves real" or
"proves fake," and we never issue a pass/fail. The recurring truths to repeat in each guide:

- **No single marker is a verdict.** Super-fakes pass individual checks; the read comes from
  many markers together.
- **A correct serial/date code is one data point, never proof** — counterfeiters copy valid
  codes off real bags.
- **Absence is not proof of a fake** — stickers fall off, vintage pieces predate serials,
  cards get lost.
- **We are not an authentication service.** For anything high-value, refer to a professional
  (Entrupy, Real Authentication, a reputable reseller's guarantee), the same "refer, not act
  as agent" frame the catalog uses elsewhere.
- **Community nicknames are labels, never the house's official name.**

NEVER-INVENT held throughout: every marker below carries a source and a check-date. Where a
detail is community/forum consensus rather than a house fact, it says so and is labeled
lower-confidence. Brands with thin sourcing are flagged in the closing note.

## CHANEL

**Markers worth checking**
1. **Serial sticker / microchip (era-dependent).** Pre-2021 bags carry a printed serial
   sticker inside (often on a small leather tab or interior corner); 2021 onward Chanel
   replaced it with an embedded NFC metal microchip plate and dropped the authenticity card —
   know which era you're in before you start. *(codogirl; Fashionphile; archive chanel.md;
   reseller-authenticator + house-fact on the cutover.)*
2. **Serial digit count vs claimed age.** 7-digit serials run ~1986–2005, 8-digit ~2005–2021;
   the leading digit(s) place the year-range, so a digit count that contradicts the bag's
   claimed age is a flag — but the range only dates it, it does not name the collection.
   *(archive chanel.md Table A, cross-checked Xupes + Couture USA + Fashionphile.)*
3. **Sticker security features.** An authentic pre-2021 sticker combines iridescent speckles,
   tiny CC markings, and an "X" scored into the laminate to resist peeling; "CHANEL" printed
   across the top/bottom (rather than the side) is a known counterfeit tell. *(codogirl,
   reseller-authenticator.)*
4. **Microchip plate execution.** On chip-era bags the metal plate's color should match the
   bag's hardware exactly, corners are slightly rounded, and the laser CC + 8-character code is
   so shallow your fingertip glides over it (you feel it only with a nail). *(codogirl,
   reseller-authenticator.)*
5. **CC lock overlap geometry.** On the turn-lock the right C overlaps the left at the top and
   the left overlaps the right at the bottom, each C equal in width with flat (not rounded)
   edges. *(codogirl, reseller-authenticator.)*
6. **Hardware screws + "Made in" stamp.** Modern lock plates use Chanel's six-point star screws
   (vintage = flathead); Phillips/X-head screws are wrong, and the stamp reads "Made in France"
   or "Made in Italy" — never "Made in Paris." *(codogirl, reseller-authenticator.)*
7. **Quilt alignment + stitch density.** On a Classic Flap the back-pocket quilting lines up
   with the body, and quilted edges typically run ~10+ stitches per diamond side (varies by
   model/size). *(codogirl, reseller-authenticator.)*

**Most-faked model:** the Classic Flap (11.12 / A01112) — the single most-checked Chanel.

**Era cutoffs:** serial sticker + authenticity card era ≈ mid-1980s→2021 (serial series 1→31).
**April 2021: NFC microchip replaces the sticker, card discontinued; the chip holds a random
code with no year and no series.** 2021 is an overlap year (either format). On a chip bag the
**retail price tag with QR is the only on-item way to read the season** (e.g. 18C = Cruise
2018) — the season code lives on the tag/RTW label, never in the chip. *(archive chanel.md era
section.)*

**What we explicitly DON'T claim:** we don't read a microchip (only Chanel's in-store NFC can
query their database), and we don't treat a present serial or card as proof — a clean number is
necessary, not sufficient.

## HERMÈS

**Markers worth checking**
1. **Blind stamp shape by era.** The discreet heat stamp (a letter + a shape) dates the bag:
   no shape pre-1971, circle 1971–1996, square 1997–2014, then no shape again from late 2014
   (T onward) — a stamp that breaks this system, or sits in an odd spot, warrants scrutiny.
   *(retyche; reseller-authenticator.)*
2. **Where the stamp lives.** On a Birkin it's under the front flap on the left side of the
   strap near the body; on a Kelly under the front flap near the turn-lock — small (~2–3mm),
   pressed without ink, deliberately hard to find. *(retyche, reseller-authenticator.)*
3. **Craftsman/atelier stamp.** A separate small artisan mark sits near the blind stamp (one
   maker builds and repairs the whole bag); fakes often omit it entirely. *(retyche,
   reseller-authenticator.)*
4. **Saddle (sellier) stitching.** Hand saddle-stitch with two needles per hole gives a slightly
   raised, even, left-leaning diagonal in waxed linen thread — perfectly straight, uniform
   machine stitching is a red flag. *(retyche, reseller-authenticator.)*
5. **Hardware engraving + zipper behaviour.** "HERMÈS PARIS" engraving is crisp and evenly
   spaced; plating shouldn't peel to a different color; an authentic zipper pull stays parallel
   to the zip line rather than flopping to 90°. *(retyche, reseller-authenticator.)*
6. **Lock/key numbers match.** The padlock is cleanly engraved "Hermès," and its number matches
   both keys; commonly faked lock numbers include 121, 212, 102. *(retyche,
   reseller-authenticator.)*
7. **Exotic blind-stamp symbol.** Exotics carry an extra mark by the stamp — square = alligator,
   two dots = Niloticus croc, caret (^) = Porosus croc, dash = lizard. *(retyche + archive
   hermes.md leather table; reseller + established beat — re-confirm against an auction catalog
   before publishing, lower confidence on exact symbols.)*

**Most-faked model:** the Birkin (then the Kelly) — the highest-stakes single check in the
market.

**Era cutoffs:** the blind-stamp **shape** changes are the era spine (see marker 1). Hermès
does **not** use serial numbers or a public date-code lookup, so the stamp + craftsmanship are
the on-item record. Leather availability also dates a bag (e.g. Togo from 1997, Epsom replaced
Courchevel in 2004, Barenia Faubourg from 2016) — useful corroboration, per archive hermes.md.

**What we explicitly DON'T claim:** the blind stamp dates and corroborates; it does not by
itself authenticate, and we don't publish leather/exotic-symbol calls as verdicts. Birkin/Kelly
purchases belong with a professional.

## DIOR

**Markers worth checking**
1. **Tag/serial system by era.** Pre-1990s pieces have woven fabric labels and often no date
   code; 1990s–2000s added sewn leather tabs + interior stampings; 2000s–2010s used serial
   stickers on authenticity cards; 2019+ integrates microchips with serials inside pockets or on
   leather tabs — match the format to the production era. *(fashionica; reseller-authenticator.)*
2. **Oblique canvas alignment.** On the genuine jacquard the D-I-O-R motifs align cleanly across
   seams and curves with a sharp, consistent repeat; fakes most often fail at the seams (and on
   the Saddle's curved seam especially). *(fashionica, reseller-authenticator.)*
3. **"D.I.O.R." charm weight + engraving.** The individual letter charms on the Lady Dior are
   heavy and substantial with sharp, uniform engraving; hollow/lightweight charms are a flag
   (note: super-fakes now add weight, so cross-check). *(fashionica, reseller-authenticator.)*
4. **Cannage quilting depth.** On the Lady Dior the diamond quilting is deep and even in depth
   and tension across the whole bag — shallow or uneven sections warrant scrutiny. *(fashionica,
   reseller-authenticator.)*
5. **"Christian Dior" heat stamp.** Crisply impressed serif stamp with consistent spacing and
   weight, pressed into (not surface-printed on) the leather; the charm's leather backing reads
   simply "Christian Dior." *(fashionica; codogirl; reseller-authenticator.)*
6. **Country of origin.** Authentic Dior is made in France or Italy — a "Made in China" label is
   a definitive red flag. *(fashionica, reseller-authenticator.)*
7. **Zipper branding.** Pulls are branded "Christian Dior" or quality Lampo/YKK and glide
   smoothly; unbranded or snagging zippers are a flag. *(fashionica, reseller-authenticator.)*

**Most-faked model:** the Lady Dior (with the Saddle and Book Tote close behind).

**Era cutoffs:** date codes did not exist in the early 1990s (Galliano era introduced them);
**2019 onward many Lady Dior / Book Tote pieces carry a microchip**, so a newer bag verified
"digitally" with no visible serial sticker can still be legitimate. *(fashionica.)*

**What we explicitly DON'T claim:** a correct-looking interior tag is one data point, not a
verdict — counterfeiters replicate fonts, holograms, and cards. We don't authenticate the chip
ourselves.

## PRADA

**Markers worth checking**
1. **Triangle logo plaque text.** The enamel triangle reads "PRADA" with "MILANO" (never
   "Milan") and "DAL 1913," evenly spaced and legible. *(bagreligion; reseller-authenticator.)*
2. **The "R" notch.** On the plaque the "R" typically has a small notch/curl in the leg; some
   genuine vintage pieces lack it, so read it as era context, not a binary. *(bagreligion;
   fashionica; reseller-authenticator.)*
3. **Interior rope/jacquard lining logo.** On the woven interior "PRADA" the "R" does NOT carry
   the plaque notch and the "A" does not overhang; the font is sans serif and "Prada" prints
   upside-down on alternating rows. *(bagreligion, reseller-authenticator.)*
4. **Lining material.** Authentic linings are embossed jacquard nylon or Nappa leather, not a
   cheap flat synthetic. *(bagreligion, reseller-authenticator.)*
5. **Angled stitch pattern.** Prada uses a distinctive neat, even *angled* topstitch; plain
   horizontal topstitching, uneven holes, or thin thread are common fake tells. *(bagreligion,
   reseller-authenticator.)*
6. **Hardware + zipper brands.** Hardware is one consistent tone (Prada doesn't mix gold/silver
   on a bag) and zippers come from Lampo, YKK, Riri, Opti, or Ipi. *(bagreligion;
   reseller-authenticator.)*
7. **Quality-assurance tag.** Most bags have a small white QA tag sewn into the interior pocket
   seam with a black factory number — cross-reference the card's serial/style separately.
   *(bagreligion, reseller-authenticator.)*

**Most-faked model:** the nylon (re-edition) shoulder bag / backpack line — the Prada the most
people check, especially with the nylon revival.

**Era cutoffs:** the **R-notch** is the main era variable (some vintage plaques lack it).
Authenticity cards are a retail inclusion, not a guarantee — copyable. *(bagreligion.)*

**What we explicitly DON'T claim:** the triangle plaque is the most-copied element, so a
correct-looking plaque alone proves nothing; styles and seasons vary in finish, so we present
markers to weigh, not a checklist that "passes" a bag.

## GOYARD  *(addition)*

**Markers worth checking**
1. **Chevron Y's touch at the dots.** On hand-screened Goyardine the interlocking Y's meet — the
   end-dots of each Y touch the neighbour; visible gaps between Y's are the fastest tell.
   *(fashionica; collectorscage; reseller + forum, consistent.)*
2. **Canvas feel + layered depth.** Authentic Goyardine is coated linen/cotton with a slightly
   raised, textured hand and depth from three overlapping color layers; a flat one-or-two-color,
   plasticky/vinyl surface is a flag. *(fashionica, reseller-authenticator.)*
3. **Heat stamp execution.** "GOYARD / PARIS / MADE IN FRANCE" is thin, even, and *shallow* and
   easy to read; stamps pressed too deep, too thick, or crowded are a tell. *(fashionica,
   reseller-authenticator.)*
4. **Weight (counter-intuitive).** A genuine Saint Louis is surprisingly *light* (coated linen);
   a heavy, stiff tote suggests the vinyl/PVC counterfeiters use. *(fashionica,
   reseller-authenticator.)*
5. **Serial format + location.** Three letters + six digits (e.g. ABC 123456), thin discreet
   sans-serif, location by model (Saint Louis/Anjou: pochette flap or strap; Artois: leather tab
   behind the interior pocket). *(fashionica, reseller-authenticator.)*
6. **Stitching.** Thin, long, dense stitches (~8–10 per inch); on classic wallets the five
   parallel rows are straight, evenly spaced, and consistent. *(fashionica,
   reseller-authenticator.)*
7. **No authenticity card exists.** Goyard issues no authenticity card — a seller offering one
   "as proof" is a red flag in itself. *(fashionica, reseller-authenticator.)*

**Most-faked model:** the Saint Louis tote — one of the most-counterfeited bags in resale.

**Era cutoffs:** **no public date-code system and no card** — Goyard's only on-item identifiers
are the heat stamp and serial, so font/depth/execution matter more than the digits. Made in
France only; "Made in China" on the stamp is definitive. *(fashionica.)*

**What we explicitly DON'T claim:** there's no official serial lookup, so we don't decode a
Goyard serial to a date; the digits are one data point, the canvas/stamp execution is the read.

## SAINT LAURENT / YSL  *(addition)*

**Markers worth checking**
1. **Brand name must match the era (the single biggest tell).** Pre-2012 interior stamps read
   "Yves Saint Laurent"; 2012–~2023 "Saint Laurent Paris" (Hedi Slimane rebrand); recent bags
   "SAINT LAURENT." A post-2012 bag stamped "Yves Saint Laurent" (or vice versa) is a red flag.
   *(fashionica; reseller-authenticator.)*
2. **Cassandre (YSL) hardware engraving.** The interlocking Y-S-L on clasps/turn-locks is crisp,
   deep, symmetrical, and centered; blurry, over-rounded, or off-center letters are a flag.
   *(fashionica; lovethatbagetc; reseller-authenticator.)*
3. **"Made in Italy" stamp format.** Interior reads the era-correct brand name then "MADE IN
   ITALY" in all caps with correct spacing. *(fashionica, reseller-authenticator.)*
4. **Serial embossed, not printed.** Serials (≈6–12 digits, sometimes letter-suffixed for
   color/material) are pressed into the leather — surface-printed/painted numbers are a flag; no
   official public lookup exists. *(fashionica, reseller-authenticator + forum.)*
5. **Quilting on Loulou/Envelope.** Diamond-quilted grain de poudre has consistent depth, even
   alignment, and identical diamond sizes; shallow/uneven quilting is a tell. *(fashionica,
   reseller-authenticator.)*
6. **Hardware weight + zipper brand.** Brass-based hardware feels substantial (hollow/rattling is
   a flag); zippers are Lampo, Riri, or Éclair with branded pulls. *(fashionica,
   reseller-authenticator.)*
7. **Edge finishing.** Clean, painted leather edges; rough/fraying edges indicate inferior
   production. *(fashionica, reseller-authenticator.)*

**Most-faked model:** the Loulou (with the Kate close behind).

**Era cutoffs:** **2012 rebrand** is the master cutoff — "Yves Saint Laurent" → "Saint Laurent
Paris" → "SAINT LAURENT." Some recent Saint Laurent releases carry NFC authentication chips.
*(fashionica.)*

**What we explicitly DON'T claim:** the rebrand is a production-era distinction, not by itself a
fake indicator; we don't decode serials to dates (no public database), and a plausible serial
with poor embossing is still suspect.

## BOTTEGA VENETA  *(addition)*

**Markers worth checking**
1. **Intrecciato held by compression, not thread (the key test).** On genuine BV the woven leather
   strips are pressed and held by compression with no stitching running along the weave edges;
   visible thread holding the strips is the single most common fake tell. *(fashionica;
   reseller-authenticator.)*
2. **Strip uniformity + flat drape.** Strips are even in width (~1.2cm on classics) and the weave
   lies flat across corners and seams; bubbling, lifting, gaps, or a puffy look are flags.
   *(fashionica, reseller-authenticator.)*
3. **Blind-debossed heat stamp.** "BOTTEGA VENETA / MADE IN ITALY" is delicately debossed into
   leather/suede (the "Made in Italy" line thinner and smaller), almost blending in — a sharp,
   printed-looking stamp is wrong, as is any loud external logo on a "no-logo" house.
   *(fashionica, reseller-authenticator.)*
4. **Serial flag by era.** A small sewn-in seam tag carries an era-specific code; the
   2001–late-2010s tag packs model/leather/color/year/batch/serial (e.g. "114076 VCHA1 2640"),
   Daniel-Lee-era tags often have a raw frayed edge — match the format to the era. *(fashionica,
   reseller-authenticator.)*
5. **Leather hand + scent.** Butter-soft calfskin/lambskin, supple with a slight matte finish and
   genuine leather scent; plasticky shine, board-stiff hand, or chemical smell are flags.
   *(fashionica, reseller-authenticator.)*
6. **Hardware + zipper.** Substantial, often brushed hardware; zippers frequently Riri/Lampo with
   crisp engraving — flimsy metal or unbranded snagging zippers are flags. *(fashionica,
   reseller-authenticator.)*
7. **Made in Italy.** BV leather bags are made in Italy; "Made in China" on a leather bag is a
   strong fake indicator. *(fashionica, reseller-authenticator.)*

**Most-faked model:** the Cassette and the Pouch (Daniel Lee era), with the Jodie rising.

**Era cutoffs:** pre-2001 vintage often has **no serial at all** (normal, not a flag); the
2001–late-2010s coded paper-fabric tag → the Daniel-Lee-era (2018+) unique-serial tag with raw
edge; Teen sizing arrived early 2022, so a small bag isn't automatically fake. *(fashionica.)*

**What we explicitly DON'T claim:** with no monogram, we lean on the weave and leather, not a
logo; a missing serial on a modern bag is a yellow flag to investigate, not a verdict, and many
genuine vintage pieces never had one.

## CELINE  *(addition)*

**Markers worth checking**
1. **Accent aigu over the E (era tell).** Philo-era stamps read "CÉLINE" *with* the accent; Hedi
   Slimane's 2018 rebrand dropped it to "CELINE" — so a 2010s Phantom/Luggage/Box should carry
   the accent (pieces produced just after the Sept 4, 2018 change may lack it). *(TheRealReal
   RealStyle; reseller-authenticator.)*
2. **Date code format + location.** Philo-era codes follow the LV-style alphanumeric (season
   letter + 2-letter workshop + 4 digits where 1st/3rd = month, 2nd/4th = year, e.g. 0164 = June
   2014); location is consistent within a style (Luggage: interior pocket; Trapeze: back pocket;
   Trio: interior leather edge by the zipper). *(TheRealReal; reseller-authenticator.)*
3. **Stamp finish.** Brand stamp is foil matched to the hardware color or a blind deboss (no
   foil), correct sans-serif font, centered — an oversized logo (to "look obvious") is a fake
   tell. *(TheRealReal, reseller-authenticator.)*
4. **Triomphe lettering precision.** On the Triomphe the logo lettering (the accent and the
   letterforms) is exact and evenly weighted; counterfeiters routinely miss the accent detail and
   letter proportions. *(aliceelizabethluxury; reseller + community, slightly lower confidence.)*
5. **Exterior vs interior stamp wording.** The outside stamp reads "CELINE PARIS" and the
   interior carries the "Made in Italy" mark — wording/placement that's swapped or off is a flag.
   *(yololuxuryconsignment; reseller-authenticator.)*
6. **Logo-to-hardware color match.** The stamp foil should match the hardware tone (gold stamp
   with gold hardware, etc.). *(yololuxuryconsignment, reseller-authenticator.)*

**Most-faked model:** the Triomphe (modern Slimane era) and the Philo-era Luggage Tote / Box.

**Era cutoffs:** **2018 rebrand** is the master cutoff — "Céline" (accent, Philo) →
"CELINE" (no accent, Slimane); date codes follow the LV format through the Philo years.
*(TheRealReal.)*

**What we explicitly DON'T claim:** the accent dates the era, it doesn't prove authenticity; we
don't decode a date code as proof (the format is copyable), and we flag the Triomphe-lettering
point as the lower-confidence one to verify on close photos.

## BALENCIAGA  *(addition — the City/First family)*

**Markers worth checking**
1. **Lampo zipper engraving.** Authentic moto-bag zippers carry the *Lampo* logo on the back of
   each zip head — embossed (not engraved), in italics, underlined with a "lightning bolt"; fakes
   often render it thick and clumsy. (Note: some early bags used engraved logos; 2014+ replaced
   the Lampo mark with an uppercase "B," and 2010 rose-gold hardware briefly lacked it.)
   *(lovethatbagetc; reseller-authenticator.)*
2. **Notched rivets.** From 2005 (consistently by F/W) rivets are deeply notched; pre-2005 were
   rounded/convex — fakes often show shallow, squarish, or half-moon notches. *(lovethatbagetc,
   reseller-authenticator.)*
3. **Bale shape.** The metal bale at the strap end has a rounded, organic shape tapering to a
   smooth flat end — abrupt "coat-hanger" angles are a fake tell. *(lovethatbagetc,
   reseller-authenticator.)*
4. **Tag letter code vs claimed season.** Silver/leather interior tags use a single letter for
   season/year (the alphabet reset, so e.g. "D" = F/W 2003 *or* S/S 2016 — but the tag font/style
   differs by era); the style number on the tag front must match the back. *(lovethatbagetc,
   reseller-authenticator.)*
5. **Silver "925" tag stamp (early classic-hardware bags).** City/First/Work/Weekender with
   classic hardware had genuine silver tags stamped "925" in the bottom-right corner through F/W
   2008. *(lovethatbagetc, reseller-authenticator.)*
6. **"Made in Italy" heat-stamp evolution.** The leather-tag back changed to uppercase "MADE IN
   ITALY" in 2011, the season letter was added to the tag back in 2012, and "FABRIQUÉ EN ITALIE"
   was added in 2014 — so the tag-back text should match the production era. *(lovethatbagetc,
   reseller-authenticator.)*

**Most-faked model:** the City (and the First) — the classic Balenciaga fake battleground; the
"C" tag is among the most copied.

**Era cutoffs:** letter-code alphabet began F/W 2003 and has reset once; rivets changed ~2005;
tag-back wording changed 2011 / 2012 / 2014; Lampo → "B" in 2014. Pre-2012 leather-tag-only
bags are hardest to place and need seasonal-color/font knowledge. *(lovethatbagetc.)*

**What we explicitly DON'T claim:** Balenciaga is openly "one of the most complicated to
authenticate, with multiple exceptions to most rules" — so every marker here is a weigh-point
with documented exceptions, never a standalone verdict, and the most-faked "C" tag means a
correct-looking tag proves nothing on its own.

# ROUND 2 — FENDI and LOEWE

*Appended 2026-06-30. The two fastest-rising brands in Entrupy's 2026 report (Fendi +49%,
Loewe +45%) — the round-2 pair flagged in Part 1. Same format and bar as above. Both are
honestly thinner than the round-1 nine, for different reasons noted per brand. All markers

applies here too.*

---

## FENDI  *(round 2)*

*Reality check: Fendi authentication overlaps the LV/Gucci logo-canvas playbook — the FF/Zucca
print and the metal FF logo are the spine, and those are exactly what counterfeiters invest in
most, so the canvas alone is weak. The stronger signal is execution (print registration, logo
geometry, hardware, stitching) plus the serial-era timeline.*

**Markers worth checking**
1. **Serial/tag by era.** Pre-1980s pieces often have **no serial at all**; 1980s–2000s use a
   serial leather tag, and a **"Fendi SAS"** marking is a reliable older-era indicator;
   2000s-onward added hologram tags; recent models add QR codes / NFC chips for digital
   verification — match the format to the claimed age. *(fashionica; reseller-authenticator.)*
2. **FF logo geometry.** The double-F is sharp-edged, symmetrical, and holds a specific
   width-to-height ratio that fakes routinely miss; on Zucca-print pieces the two F's sit
   slightly **offset** on either side of the rectangle (never perfectly mirror-aligned), and
   the print should never look elongated or stretched. *(fashionica; 9ine Life; reseller +
   reference site.)*
3. **Zucca canvas tone + registration.** Genuine Zucca canvas has a tight weave, clean print
   registration, and a **warm tone — it should never read green**; loose weave or color bleed
   is a flag. *(fashionica; TheRealReal; reseller-authenticator.)*
4. **Hardware engraving + screws.** "FENDI" engraving on hardware is crisp and properly deep
   (not shallow/surface-etched); on the Baguette clasp backing the screws are flat-head — a
   Phillips or star screw is a tell. *(fashionica; reseller-authenticator.)*
5. **Zipper brand + feel.** Fendi uses quality YKK, Lampo, or branded-Fendi zippers that glide;
   flimsy, catching, or unbranded zippers are a flag. *(fashionica; reseller-authenticator.)*
6. **"Made in Italy" stamp, impressed.** The origin stamp is pressed into the leather (not
   surface-printed) in the correct font — and it reads **"Made in Italy" only**; "Made in Roma"
   or any other country is a definitive fake tell (note: "Fendi Roma" appears as a brand mark,
   which is different from the origin line). *(fashionica; reseller-authenticator.)*
7. **Lining sewn, not glued.** Fabric/suede/leather lining (commonly brown, beige, burgundy by
   era) is neatly sewn in with no bunching; glued lining or exposed raw edges are a flag.
   *(fashionica; reseller-authenticator.)*

**Most-faked model:** the Baguette (the most-counterfeited Fendi), with the Peekaboo close
behind. *(fashionica.)*

**Era cutoffs:** **no serial** (pre-1980s) → **serial tag / "Fendi SAS"** (1980s–2000s) →
**hologram tag** (2000s+) → **QR code / NFC chip** (2020s+, scannable via the official Fendi
app). The hologram should shift color when tilted; a flat hologram is a flag, but a present
hologram is not proof. *(fashionica.)*

**What we explicitly DON'T claim:** the FF/Zucca print and a correct-looking hologram or serial
are the most-copied elements, so none of them proves authenticity on its own; we don't scan or
decode the NFC chip, and the read comes from execution across many markers, not the logo.

## LOEWE  *(round 2)*

*Reality check (state it plainly in the guide): Loewe has no publicly decodable date code and
**no consumer-facing online verification tool** — any site claiming an official Loewe lookup is
a scam. So this guide leans harder on **craft judgment** than on a code: the embossed Anagram,
the leather hand, and Puzzle/Hammock construction. Several markers below are explicitly
craft-judgment, not hard binary tells, and are flagged as such.*

**Markers worth checking**
1. **Anagram (four interlocking L's) symmetry — the primary checkpoint.** The four L's must be
   identical in weight, size, and spacing and centered; asymmetry, thick-thin variation, or an
   off-center mark is the most common fake tell. *(fashionica; TheRealReal; reseller +
   community, consistent.)*
2. **The "O" in LOEWE.** On the wordmark the O is **not a perfect circle** — it has slightly
   sharp/pointed ends (a subtle "football" shape); a perfectly round O is a known counterfeit
   tell. *(fashionica; reseller — single-strong-source, medium confidence, verify on close
   photos.)*
3. **Leather hand (craft judgment).** Authentic Nappa calfskin is exceptionally soft, supple,
   fine-grained, and slightly heavy; papery, stiff, or plasticky leather — or an excessively
   uniform "machine" grain — is a flag. *This is feel-based, not a hard marker — call it
   craft judgment.* *(fashionica; TheRealReal — TRR notes fakes often show a larger grain than
   the real bag.)*
4. **Puzzle panel geometry + seam lines.** On the Puzzle the geometric leather panels fit with
   precision (no gaps, overlaps, or misaligned edges) and the seam lines between panels are
   **thick, bold, and clearly defined** — thin seam lines that fade into the leather are a tell;
   the knotted strap attachments should be tight and identical on both sides. *(fashionica;
   reseller-authenticator.)*
5. **Hardware Anagram engraving + zipper.** On metal hardware the Anagram is deeply, precisely
   engraved and **fully fills the palladium/gold square** (a logo floating with big margins is a
   flag); zippers are Lampo or Riri and the puller's LOEWE logo is thin with wide letter
   spacing. *(fashionica; reseller-authenticator.)*
6. **Interior tag execution + serial debossed.** The interior leather tab carries "LOEWE" over
   "Made in Spain" over the serial; it should have **defined (not fully rounded) corners**, feel
   as good as the bag, and the serial must be **debossed into the leather, never surface-printed**
   — and the "Made in Spain" line is deliberately bolder than the "LOEWE" above it. *(fashionica;
   legitique; reseller-authenticator.)*
7. **Stamp depth — delicate, not hammered.** The Made-in-Spain/serial stamp and exterior Anagram
   emboss are applied with a **light, delicate touch**; a stamp pressed far too hard/deep is a
   replica tell (fakes tend to over-press). *(legitique; community/PurseForum — corroborated,
   medium confidence.)*

**Most-faked model:** the Puzzle (the viral 2015+ icon and the most-counterfeited Loewe), with
the Hammock next. *(fashionica.)*

**Era cutoffs:** there is **no serial/date lookup** — the serial is a date/factory code, not a
unique bag ID (the same number recurs across a production run; the 3rd–4th digits *generally*
indicate the year — treat as a soft hint, not proof). The one clean era line is the **2013
Jonathan Anderson redesign of the Anagram** — pre-2013 (e.g. vintage Amazona) carries a
different logo structure, so research era-specific logos before calling one "wrong." Pre-2013
pieces may also predate consistent serialing; authenticate those on craft alone. Mainline
handbags are **Made in Spain** (90%+ local production); a non-Spain origin on a current handbag
is a strong flag. *(fashionica.)*

**What we explicitly DON'T claim:** much of Loewe authentication is craft judgment (leather
hand, stamp "feel", Anagram proportions), not a code you can verify — so we present it as
weigh-points, flag the feel-based ones as judgment, and never imply a serial can be looked up to
prove a bag. There is no Loewe authenticity card; its absence is not a flag.
