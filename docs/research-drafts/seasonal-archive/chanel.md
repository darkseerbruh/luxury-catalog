# Chanel — season-code & serial-series map

*Archivist deliverable. Early Task 2 of the seasonal naming archive. Built 2026-06-28.
This is reference data other work will trust, so where sources are approximate it says so
and lowers confidence. No code, letter, or year here is invented; every row carries a source.*

## What this maps (read first — there are TWO systems, do not conflate them)

Chanel encodes "when" in two completely separate places, and the market loosely calls both
a "date code." They are not the same thing:

1. **Serial series number** (the interior sticker / authenticity-card number). A running
   1-to-32 "series" that increments roughly with production years. This is what you read off
   a pre-2021 bag's sticker to date it. It does NOT tell you the collection (Cruise vs Fall etc).
   It ended at series 31 in 2021 when Chanel moved to microchips. See **Table A**.
2. **Season code** — the `[YY][letter]` form (17B, 18C, 23S, 26A). This lives on the **retail
   price tag / hangtag and the garment/RTW label**, not in the serial sticker. The two digits
   are the year; the letter is the collection (Cruise, Spring Act 1, etc). This is the system
   the catalog uses to say "Cruise 2018." See the **Letter legend** and **Table B**.

Key consequence: a bag's serial sticker dates it to a year *range*; its season code (when a
tag survives) names the exact collection. Post-2021 microchip bags carry **no** year in the
chip, so the **retail tag is the only on-item source of the season** (Fashionphile, verified).

## Letter legend (the `[YY][letter]` season codes)

Modern regime (the six collections Chanel runs per year). Cross-checked across PurseForum,
PurseBop, and Coco Approved — all three agree:

| Letter | Collection | French / full name | Typical release | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| C | Cruise / Resort | Croisière | November of the **prior** year | high |
| P | Spring / Summer Act 1 | Printemps | January | high |
| S | Summer / Spring-Summer Act 2 | (Été) | March | high |
| A | Métiers d'Art *(modern)* | — | May | high |
| B | Fall / Winter Act 1 | — | July | high |
| K | Fall / Winter Act 2 | — | September | high |
| M | Coco Beach (seasonal beach capsule) | — | varies (resort capsule) | medium |

Notes that matter (sourced, hedged where the record is thin):

- **The "A" trap — vintage A ≠ modern A.** On older RTW labels (roughly the 1990s–2000s,
  e.g. `97A`, `05A`, `08A`), **A meant Automne (Autumn / Fall)**, the main Fall-Winter
  collection — not Métiers d'Art. A PurseForum collector documents a 2005 Fall runway piece
  labeled `05A`. The `B` and `K` Fall split (Act 1 / Act 2) is the modern convention; vintage
  Fall pieces were commonly just `A`. So read `A` by era: **Fall** on a vintage tag, **Métiers
  d'Art** on a modern one. Confidence: medium (forum-documented, not a house publication).
- **Cruise (C) is dated to the show year, released the prior November.** 23C showed/released
  Nov 2022 but is the 2023 Cruise collection. This is consistent across PurseBop and PurseForum.
- **P vs S** both fall under "Spring/Summer": P is Act 1 (the January / main SS runway), S is
  Act 2 (the March follow-on / "Summer"). Some older references collapse both into one
  Spring-Summer; treat the Act 1 / Act 2 split as the post-~2010s convention.
- **M = Coco Beach** is the seasonal beach/swim capsule (forum-confirmed via 24M-tagged items).
  It is a capsule, not one of the six core runway collections — present but lower-confidence.
- Letters are not invented to fill gaps. If a tag shows a letter not in this legend, log it as
  "unmapped, verify," never guess.

## Era cutover (the 2021 line)

- **Sticker + authenticity-card era:** mid-1980s → 2021. Serial series 1→31. First digit(s)
  ≈ production year; season letter (when present) on the RTW/retail tag.
- **Microchip (NFC metal plate) era:** April 2021 onward. The plate holds a random 8-character
  alphanumeric code with **no year and no series**; it is read only by Chanel's in-store NFC
  equipment. Authenticity cards were discontinued. **2021 is the overlap year** — a bag may
  carry either a final 31-series sticker or a microchip depending on production batch.
- **Practical rule:** for a microchip bag, the **retail price tag (with QR) is the only
  on-item way to read the season code.** No tag, no on-item season; fall back to the runway
  archive / reseller listing to place the collection. (Fashionphile + Xupes, both verified.)

## What I could NOT source (honest gaps)

- **No house-published reference exists.** Chanel has never officially published either the
  series-number system or the season-letter legend (Xupes states this explicitly). Everything
  here is the reseller/authenticator/forum consensus, which is why no row claiming an exact
  year is rated above what the cross-check supports, and serial→year stays as ranges.
- **Pre-1996 season letters** are thin: vintage RTW labels are inconsistent and many early
  bags carry only a serial sticker, no season letter. The 96A–00A rows are the documented
  vintage-A pattern, not a complete year-by-year capture; treat as medium.
- **Exact series→year boundaries** wobble ±1 year between sources (batch production, regional
  releases). Presented as ranges on purpose. A false-precise single year would be the error.
- **The "A" disambiguation date** (when A flipped from Fall to Métiers d'Art only) is not
  pinned to a single year in any source; the ~2012 boundary in Table B is an informed estimate,
  hence medium confidence on those rows.
