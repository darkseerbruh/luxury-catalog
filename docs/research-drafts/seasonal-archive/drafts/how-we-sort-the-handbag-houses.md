## The short answer (front-loaded for the GEO snippet)

Luxury Catalog sorts handbag houses into four tiers: **ultra-luxury, luxury, premium, and
contemporary.** We sort by two things. First, the **resale-price band** a house's bags
typically command, because resale, not retail, is where a bag's market-tested standing
actually shows. Second, **prestige and heritage** as the tiebreaker when two houses sit in
the same band.

A tier is a guide, not a verdict, and no tier is "better." They answer different questions.
Ultra-luxury answers "will this hold its value." Contemporary answers "can I get a
beautifully designed bag without spending four figures." Most people want a different answer
on different days.

So, to the question people actually search: yes, Gucci is a luxury brand. In our framework
it sits in the **premium** tier, a real leather-goods house whose icon bags resell strongly,
just a band below the houses whose bags routinely hold or gain value. More on why below.

## How we sort, and why resale

Retail price tells you what a house wants for a bag. Resale tells you what the market will
actually pay for it once the boutique glow wears off. That gap is the whole game. A bag can
launch at a high retail number and lose half its value the day it leaves the store, or it can
resell above retail years later. Resale is the honest signal, so it leads our sort. Heritage
and prestige break the ties.

Two notes on the numbers before the tiers. **Value-retention figures are an estimate, not an
appraisal**, and they move with the market. Every one below traces to the **Rebag 2025 Clair
Report** (reported via PR Newswire and Fashionista, December 2025), which measures resale
value as a percentage of original retail. **Resale bands are typical ranges, not quotes** on
any specific bag. The right bag in the right leather and condition can sit well outside its
house's usual band.

## The four tiers

### Ultra-luxury: The bags that hold or gain value

The pinnacle heritage houses. The defining trait is not the retail price, it is that the bags
hold their value, and the best of them gain it. The Rebag 2025 Clair Report calls a bag that
retains 85% or more of its retail value a "unicorn," and these houses produce them routinely.

By the Rebag 2025 figures, [Hermès](/brand/hermes) retains about **138%** of retail (the bag
is worth more secondhand than new), and [Goyard](/brand/goyard) about **132%**. Top
[Louis Vuitton](/brand/louis-vuitton) styles run **above 130%**. [The Row](/brand/the-row),
a new entrant in the 2025 report, lands near **97%**. [Chanel](/brand/chanel) belongs here on
heritage and standing, even as its retail hikes have reshaped the resale math.

If your question is "will this still be worth something in ten years," this is the tier that
answers it. That is the estimate, not a promise. Markets turn.

### Luxury: Established houses, icon bags that hold

Established design houses whose signature bags resell around **$1,500 and up** and hold their
value well, even if they do not typically clear retail the way the ultra-luxury unicorns do.
This is confident, everyday luxury: a bag with real standing that you actually carry.

Houses here include [Dior](/brand/dior), [Bottega Veneta](/brand/bottega-veneta),
[Celine](/brand/celine), [Saint Laurent](/brand/saint-laurent), and [Loewe](/brand/loewe).

### Premium: Real leather-goods houses, higher volume

Serious leather-goods houses with broad, recognizable lineups. Typical resale runs roughly
**$700 to $1,500**, with higher production volume than the tiers above, so more of the bags
are out there and the resale floor sits lower. The craft is real; the scale is bigger.

This is where [Gucci](/brand/gucci) sits, alongside [Prada](/brand/prada),
[Fendi](/brand/fendi), [Givenchy](/brand/givenchy), [Valentino](/brand/valentino),
[Balenciaga](/brand/balenciaga), and [Miu Miu](/brand/miu-miu). A premium bag is a
design-led pick from a house you know, at a price that does not ask you to think of it as an
asset.

### Contemporary: Accessible, design-led, high volume

Accessible houses with high production volume and typical resale **under about $500**. These
bags are made to be bought and carried, not tracked as investments, and that is a feature, not
a knock. A great contemporary bag is a great bag.

Houses here include [Coach](/brand/coach), [Kate Spade](/brand/kate-spade),
[Burberry](/brand/burberry), and [Chloé](/brand/chloe). The thrift-flip world lives largely
in this tier, where a sharp eye at a resale rack can turn a real find. Fashion is subjective,
and the only wrong bag is one you do not love.

## Where the lines blur (this is the part most lists skip)

A tier is a starting point, not a cage. Here is where it gets interesting, and where a flat
"hierarchy" list will steer you wrong.

**The same house spans tiers by style.** A house lives in one tier on average, but its bags do
not. A structured, quilted [Coach](/brand/coach) Tabby in good condition resells far above a
basic Coach tote, even though both wear the same name. Tier sorts the house; the individual bag
sets its own price.

**Louis Vuitton straddles two tiers, and the sources disagree.** [Louis Vuitton](/brand/louis-vuitton)
is the clearest split. LePrix's hierarchy ("The Luxury Brand Hierarchy," July 2023) files it a
rung below Hermès and Chanel. The resale numbers argue for the very top, with top styles above
130% retention in the Rebag 2025 report. Both can be true: LV makes everyday canvas bags at
accessible prices and grail pieces that outperform almost everything. We file it as
ultra-luxury on its best styles, and we say so plainly rather than pretend the line is clean.

**Trend houses move on hype, not heritage.** Houses like [Miu Miu](/brand/miu-miu),
[Balenciaga](/brand/balenciaga), and [Jacquemus](/brand/jacquemus) can ride a wave of cultural
heat that lifts resale for a season or two, then settle. Their tier is real but less anchored
than a heritage house's, because it rests partly on momentum. That is worth knowing before you
read a hot resale number as a permanent one.

**A new creative director can re-price a house overnight.** This is the one that surprises
people. The Rebag 2025 report clocked the [Balenciaga](/brand/balenciaga) City bag up roughly
**500%** in resale interest on the news of Demna's move to Gucci. Older [Celine](/brand/celine)
bags spiked after Phoebe Philo left and her era became collectible. Leadership is a price
input, not a footnote, and it can shift a house's standing faster than any spec sheet.

## What the tier is actually for

The tier tells a shopper what **kind of decision** they are making, before they fall for a
specific bag.

- Ultra-luxury is a value-holding decision. You are buying something that has historically
  held its worth. (An estimate, not a guarantee.)
- Luxury is confident everyday luxury. Real standing, a bag you carry without ceremony.
- Premium is a design-led pick from a house you trust, priced as a bag and not an asset.
- Contemporary is accessible design, made to be enjoyed. The home of the great thrift find.

It sets your price expectations up front, so the bag you fall for does not blindside your
budget. That is the whole job. No tier outranks another on whether it is worth buying. They
just answer different questions, and the right one depends on what you are asking that day.
