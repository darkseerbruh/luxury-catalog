## The hook (de-gatekeeping, one paragraph)

Bottega Veneta does not put a visible logo on its bags. The house line is basically "when your own
initials are enough," so the woven leather is the signature instead of a stamp. That makes the model
name the main way to identify a Bottega, and the names are specific, dated, and tied to who designed
them. Here is what Bottega calls its bags, who made each one and when, plus the layer underneath the
trend captions: which color names are confirmed Bottega's own, and which are the names collectors
and resellers use that the house hasn't officially confirmed.

## The DNA: Intrecciato (and the honest origin)

Intrecciato is the woven leather that runs across every model: long strips of leather hand-plaited
into a grid. The good, non-folklore detail is the origin. It was a workaround, not a flourish.
Veneto ateliers' sewing machines could only handle thin leathers, so the house wove thin strips into
something both attractive and structurally strong. Bottega trains the technique at its own school,
the Scuola dei Maestri Pellettieri. (Source: `bottega-veneta.md` §2.)

## What Bottega calls its bags

The model names are dated and attributable to a specific designer. Bottega's recent design eras:
Tomas Maier (2001 to 2018), Daniel Lee (2018 to 2021), Matthieu Blazy (2021 to 2024), and Louise
Trotter (from January 2025). (Source: `bottega-veneta.md` Hard Rule 2 + Models table.)

| Model | What it is | Debut | Designer |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cabat | The original Intrecciato statement tote: unlined, open-top, woven leather and nothing else | 2001 or 2002 (sources differ) | Tomas Maier |
| Knot | The icon evening clutch with a signature looped-knot closure | 2001 | Tomas Maier |
| Pouch | Lee's first Bottega bag: supple calfskin gathered like a dumpling; named most-wanted product of 2019 | SS2020 | Daniel Lee |
| Jodie | The knotted-handle hobo, named after a paparazzi shot of Jodie Foster shielding herself with a big black Bottega | Resort 2020 | Daniel Lee |
| Cassette | A crossbody in the oversized Maxi Intrecciato weave; the Padded Cassette (FW2019) is the cult puffy version | Pre-fall 2019 | Daniel Lee |
| Sardine | The white-hot Blazy bag, with a rigid rod handle shaped like a gilt sardine | FW2022 (Blazy's debut) | Matthieu Blazy |
| Andiamo | A rounded calf Intrecciato bag with a brass knot on top; "andiamo" means "let's go" | SS2023 | Matthieu Blazy |
| Hop | An angular 3D-Intreccio hobo, inspired by a 2002 Bottega hobo | FW2023 | Matthieu Blazy |

The two shareable origin stories worth pulling out: **Jodie** was born unnamed and got its name after
a press photo of Jodie Foster using a large black Bottega to shield herself, and Lee then put a knot
in the hobo handle. And **Intrecciato itself** started as a fix for weak sewing machines, not a
design idea. (Source: `bottega-veneta.md` Models table, Jodie + Cabat rows; §2.)

One honesty note for the piece: the Cabat's debut year is given as 2001 by Vogue and 2002 by SACLAB,
so state it as a range rather than picking one. (Source: `bottega-veneta.md` Hard Rule 3 + Cabat row.)

## The named-color layer (handle with care)

Bottega is one of the few houses that genuinely names its colors, which is what makes the color
layer worth a section. But the confidence splits in two, and the piece should keep that split visible.

**Confirmed official:** **Parakeet**, the grassy acid green Daniel Lee reintroduced for Spring/Summer
2021. It is double-sourced as Bottega's own house color and is now part of the brand's store design
and branding, the way orange reads as Hermès. This one is safe to state as an official Bottega color.
(Source: `bottega-veneta.md` §3, the flagship official color.)

**Real names, but collector-and-reseller attributed:** the wider Bottega palette (Porridge, Fondant,
Barolo, Travertine, the "Washed" pastels and more) comes from how a major reseller, Fashionphile,
catalogs the colors. They are very likely Bottega's own names, and the live trend captions use them
the same way ("a Jodie in porridge," "a Sardine in fondant"), so they are real names in circulation.
But they were not confirmed against bottegaveneta.com for the research, so the honest frame is: these
are the names collectors and resellers use, a strong lead, not yet house-verified. Frame them that
way, do not promote them to official. (Source: `bottega-veneta.md` "Official vs descriptive" rule +
§3 + Cultural-layer read, where "porridge" and "fondant" check out as real Fashionphile names and
"chestnut" did not, so it stays an unverified descriptor.)
