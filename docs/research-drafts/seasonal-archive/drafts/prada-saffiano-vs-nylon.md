## The hook (de-gatekeeping, one paragraph)

Two things trip people up about Prada, and both are easy once someone tells you straight. First,
Prada is not one material house, it is two: the cross-hatch Saffiano leather and the elevated nylon,
and a bag's identity turns on which one it is built from. Second, the "Re-Edition" bags are named
after the year the original came out of Prada's own archive, not their size or a style code. So
"Re-Edition 1995" means a bag from the 1995 archive, full stop. Here is each one, with the one
practical tell that separates the two nylon Re-Editions people mix up most.

## DNA 1: Saffiano (the leather Prada)

Saffiano is the treated calf leather with the diagonal cross-hatch finish, scratch-resistant and
shiny. Mario Prada **invented and patented it in 1913**, which is the same "dal 1913" stamped on the
triangle plaque. The flagship Saffiano bag is the **Galleria**, debuted **2007**, a structured tote
named after the Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, the site of Prada's first 1913 store. The Cahier, the
Promenade (also called the Saffiano Lux Tote), and the Sidonie are also Saffiano bags. This is the
leather Prada. (Source: `prada.md` §2 + Models table.)

## DNA 2: Nylon (the nylon Prada)

The other DNA is nylon, the workaday fabric Miuccia Prada turned into a luxury material. She debuted
the **Vela backpack in 1984**, the first application of Prada nylon; Prada Group calls nylon "an
emblem of the brand's DNA." Resellers catalog the classic nylon as **Tessuto** (Italian for
"fabric"). Since **2019** Prada has remade it as **Re-Nylon**, a recycled version using ECONYL, a
regenerated nylon yarn made from reclaimed plastic and fishing nets. The Re-Nylon Backpack (the
renamed Vela), the Re-Edition nylon bags, and the Moon are the nylon side of the house. This is the
nylon Prada. (Source: `prada.md` §2 + the Saffiano-vs-nylon decoder.)

Both DNAs wear the same inverted-triangle plaque stamped "dal 1913," so the plaque alone does not
tell you which Prada you are holding. The material does.

One honesty note worth keeping in the piece: the older trade name "Pocone" (or "Pocono") sometimes
cited for Prada's nylon did not turn up in a usable source for the research, so it stays out as an
unverified lead. The sourced terms are Tessuto (the classic nylon) and Re-Nylon / Econyl (the
recycled version). (Source: `prada.md` §2 note.)

## The Re-Edition decoder: the number is the archive year

Prada mines its own 1990s and 2000s archive and names each revival after the year the original
debuted. The number is not a size and not a style code. It is the year. (Source: `prada.md` Models
table + the "read first" section.)

| Name | What the number means | Material | The tell |
|---|---|---|---|
| Re-Edition 1995 | A Fall 1995 archive bag, a structured top-handle reissued in 2022 | Brushed leather | Galleria-like structured shape; a Carolyn Bessette-Kennedy reference |
| Re-Edition 2000 | An early-2000s nylon shoulder bag, reprised late 2019 in recycled Re-Nylon | Nylon / Re-Nylon | **Fabric strap** |
| Re-Edition 2005 | An early-2000s nylon shoulder bag, reprised late 2019 in recycled Re-Nylon | Nylon / Re-Nylon | **Saffiano-leather strap** |

So the practical decoder between the two minis people confuse: **Re-Edition 2000 has a fabric strap;
Re-Edition 2005 has a Saffiano-leather strap.** Same archive-year naming logic, one clean difference
you can see. (Source: `prada.md`, Re-Edition 2000 / 2005 rows + the decoder.)
