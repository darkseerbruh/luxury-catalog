# Louis Vuitton — seasonal naming archive (models + lines/canvases/leathers + named colorways)

*Archivist run, 2026-06-28 (Early Task 4, first house of the big-five sweep). LV is a Regime A
house: names read `<Model> <Line/Leather qualifier>` (e.g. "Alma BB", "Speedy Bandoulière",
"Bella Mahina"). Three layers below: a Models table, a Lines/Canvases/Leathers table, and a
Named-colorways table. Every row carries a source URL and a confidence. Companion data file:
`louis-vuitton.jsonl`.*

## How to read confidence

- **high** = house fact, auction catalog, or a fact cross-checked across two independent strong
  references (e.g. a material intro year confirmed by both Xupes and Yoogi's; a debut year on the
  LV Heritage page).
- **medium** = single well-sourced reference site / reseller blog, consistent with the beat but
  not double-confirmed this run.
- **low** = single mention or established-beat knowledge not re-sourced this run; a lead to verify.

## Hard rules I held to

1. **`<Model>` and `<Line>` are separate facts.** A bag's identity is the model (Neverfull); the
   canvas/leather (Monogram, Damier Ebene, Empreinte) is a qualifier that carries its own intro
   year and its own color vocabulary. I did not collapse them.
2. **Debut years are sourced or marked low.** Where a year is from the established beat and not
   re-confirmed this run, confidence is `low` and the row says so. I did not invent a year.
3. **Two intro-year conflicts are flagged, not smoothed.** Empreinte: Xupes/Bag Religion say
   2010, Yoogi's says 2012 (the 2010 date is the leather's debut; 2012 is when the Empreinte
   *handbag* line scaled, the likely cause of the split). Vernis: Vogue + Yoogi's history say
   Marc Jacobs introduced it 1997; Yoogi's color table first lists a Vernis color (Beige) in
   1998. Both are recorded as ranges, not collapsed to one.
