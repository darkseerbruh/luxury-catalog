# Celine / Céline: seasonal naming archive (models + materials/motifs + named colors)

*Archivist run, 2026-06-28 (house 8, "other houses" queue, after Saint Laurent). Celine is a
Regime A house: it assigns official model names, so the model name IS the canonical key. Three
layers below: a Models table (with the three-era + accent attribution), a Materials/signature-motifs
table led by the Triomphe clasp/canvas, and a Named-colors section. Every row carries a source URL
and a confidence. Companion data file: `celine.jsonl`.*

## The single most important thing about Celine naming: THREE eras and a spelling change (read first)

Celine's bag canon splits cleanly across three creative directors, and the **accent on the name is
an era tell**:

1. **Phoebe Philo era, 2008-2018 — accented "Céline."** The minimalist golden age. Philo created
   the **Luggage** (Spring 2010, her first It-bag, in Nano/Micro/Mini), **Trapeze** (2010),
   **Classic Box** (2011), **Belt Bag** (Pre-Fall 2014), **Trio**, **Phantom**, **Cabas**,
   **Sangle bucket**, **Big Bag** (Winter 2017). These are "Old Céline" in collector shorthand.
   Sourced: Spotted Fashion Philo-era guide, Weekly Lux Drop discontinued guide, PurseBlog.
2. **Hedi Slimane era, 2018-2024 — accent DROPPED to "CELINE."** When Slimane arrived in 2018 he
   **removed the accent** from C(é)line and introduced a new logo based on the brand's 1960s
   typeface. He revived the **Triomphe** clasp/monogram (a 1970s Celine archive motif, the canvas
   monogram "first revealed in 1972") and built a new canon: **16 / Le 16** (his first bag, Nov 2018),
   the **Triomphe** line (debuted on the Spring 2019 runway), **Ava** (2020), **Cuir Triomphe** (2022),
   plus the **Besace** and matelassé pieces. Sourced: PurseBlog "Four Most Important Hedi Slimane
   Celine Bags," a+ Singapore Triomphe icon story, Grazia SS19.
3. **Michael Rider era, 2024-present — "CELINE" (accent stays off).** **NOTE / CORRECTION to the
   brief:** the brief named "Marco De Vincenzo" as the post-Slimane CD; the actual successor is
   **Michael Rider**, who officially succeeded Slimane in **October 2024** and showed his **first
   collection (Spring/Summer 2026) in Paris on July 6, 2025**. Rider is reviving Philo-era shapes
   (the Phantom/"New Luggage" returns in his Printemps 2026 debut) under the Slimane "CELINE"
   wordmark. Sourced: Numéro, Vogue, Le Monde, Brown Alumni Magazine. (Marco De Vincenzo is the
   creative director of **Etro**, not Celine — likely the brief's mix-up.)

So: **accented "Céline" = Philo (pre-2018)**, **unaccented "CELINE" = Slimane-onward (2018+)**.
The accent is a fast era cue, though resellers and the secondhand market use both spellings loosely.

## The honest finding on colors (the same as Dior, Gucci, and YSL, NOT Hermès/Bottega)

**Celine does NOT publish a unique per-season color-name dictionary for most bags.** Like Dior,
Gucci, and Saint Laurent (and unlike Hermès and Bottega Veneta), Celine leans on **neutrals**
(Tan, Black, Camel, Natural, White) named as plain descriptors, not a poetic seasonal-color lexicon.
The differentiating "name" on a Celine bag is the **model** and the **material/motif** (Triomphe
canvas vs calfskin, grained "Drummed" calf), far more than a color name. The one place a color name
carries weight is the house's signature **Tan / Camel** neutral on the Luggage and Triomphe, and
the **Triomphe canvas colorways** (which are described by the canvas tone + leather trim, e.g.
"Tan," not a named season color). So this file's color layer is honest and short, and says plainly
where a named seasonal color does not exist rather than inventing one. This puts Celine firmly in
the "does not name its colors" camp with Dior / Gucci / YSL.

## How to read confidence

- **high** = house/official fact (the celine.com product copy dating the 1972 monogram; an official
  appointment date), or a fact cross-checked across two independent strong references.
- **medium** = single well-sourced reference site / reseller blog, consistent with the beat but not
  double-confirmed this run.
- **low** = single mention or established-beat knowledge not re-sourced this run; a lead to verify.

I did NOT invent a single model, motif, color, or year. Where a debut year was not sourced this run,
the row says so (null + "not sourced") rather than carrying a fabricated year. Several smaller
briefed models (Conti, Romy, Folco, Tabou, Ring) did NOT surface in a usable source this run and are
logged honestly as low/unsourced leads, not fabricated.

## 3. Named colors / signatures (the honest, short layer)

**Celine does not publish a unique per-season color-name dictionary** (the Dior / Gucci / YSL pattern,
not the Hermès / Bottega one). The bag is identified by **model + material/canvas**, and the colors
are plain descriptors. So this layer captures (a) the signature neutrals Celine actually leans on,
and (b) the Triomphe-canvas tone convention. Nothing here is invented; where a seasonal color name
does not exist I say so.

### What does NOT exist (stated plainly, not invented)

- **No per-season Celine color-name lexicon.** Unlike Hermès (Étoupe 18) or Bottega (Parakeet,
  Fondant), Celine does not name its seasonal colors. Triomphe/Ava colorways are described by the
  **canvas tone + leather trim** (e.g. "Tan," "Black"), not a poetic season name. The a+ Singapore
  piece notes "seasonal renditions are aplenty in terms of colours" (e.g. pastels) but gives them no
  house color names — consistent with the finding. Do NOT log invented Celine season colors.

## Cross-house context: where Celine sits on the "names its colors" axis

This run adds Celine to the sourced cross-house contrast (see `bottega-veneta.md` / `saint-laurent.md`
for the full frame):

- **NAME their per-season colors** (a real named-color lexicon exists): **Hermès** (Étoupe 18, Rouge
  H 46…), **Bottega Veneta** (Parakeet, Fondant, Porridge…).
- **Do NOT name most per-season colors** (plain descriptors, no house lexicon): **Dior**, **Gucci**
  (Rosso Ancora the lone exception), **Chanel** (style code + season code), **Saint Laurent**, and now
  **Celine** — identified by **model + material/canvas** (Triomphe Canvas, Drummed calfskin), with the
  neutrals (Tan, Black, Camel) as plain descriptors.

So Celine is firmly in the Dior / Gucci / YSL camp. Its distinctiveness is the **three-era + accent
structure** (Philo "Céline" / Slimane+Rider "CELINE") and the **Triomphe** revival (1970s motif,
1972 canvas) as the canonical emblem — a material-and-emblem naming regime, not a color-naming one.

## Cultural-layer read (dated 2026-06-28, hedged)

- **"Old Céline" (Philo) is the live revival lane.** The Philo-era bags (Luggage, Phantom, Box,
  Trapeze) were almost all discontinued under Slimane (Trapeze 2017, Box 2023, Luggage + Belt March
  2025), which has pushed collector demand to the secondhand market hard — the discontinuation guides
  read as buy-now-before-gone urgency. My read, not a house statement: "Old Céline" is one of the
  strongest quiet-luxury resale narratives right now, and Michael Rider **reviving the Phantom/Luggage**
  ("New Luggage," Printemps 2026) confirms the house itself sees the equity in the Philo shapes.
- **The Triomphe and Ava are the live Slimane-era cores.** The Ava (2020) is repeatedly flagged as a
  near-100%-resale-value bag and the Teen Triomphe as the most popular Triomphe size. My take: among
  current Celine bags, the Triomphe (any size) and the Ava are the two the conversation is built on.
  Opinion, not a house statement.
- **The CD transition is the live unknown.** Slimane left in 2024 after a stable, brand-defining run;
  Michael Rider (ex-Celine under Philo, then Polo Ralph Lauren) took over Oct 2024 and showed his
  first collection SS2026 (July 2025). His new model names are the live thing to watch — so far he is
  reviving Philo shapes under the Slimane wordmark rather than launching a clean-slate canon.
