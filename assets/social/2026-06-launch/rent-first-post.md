## TikTok / Reel (one idea: rent to learn what you actually need)
> I was about to buy my "dream bag," a vintage Chanel in lambskin. I rented one first. Thank god.
>
> Turns out I'm rough on my bags, I throw them in the passenger seat, I walk in the rain, and lambskin would've been ruined in a month. Renting taught me what I actually need: a structured bag, a top handle, a crossbody strap. I only knew that because I tried before I bought.
>
> That's the whole tip. Renting and buying answer different questions. Buy the bag you'll carry constantly, the cost per wear drops and it's yours. Rent the bag you'd carry a few times, or the one you're not sure about yet. The test is simple: daily driver, buy it. Trying it on for size, rent it first.
>
> It's the cheapest lesson in this hobby. Learn what your life actually needs before you spend.
