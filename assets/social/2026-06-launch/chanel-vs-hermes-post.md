## TikTok / Reel (open with a hook; 60-90s explainer)
> Used is supposed to be cheaper than new. With a Chanel Flap, sure: about $11,700 new, around $6,000 used. Half off, exactly what you'd expect.
>
> But a Birkin breaks the rule. About $14,900 new, around $18,000 used. You pay more for the used one.
>
> Same "holds value" reputation, opposite math. And it's not because Chanel is cheap. Chanel actually raised its price faster, the Flap nearly doubled in five years.
>
> The difference is the door. You can walk into Chanel and buy a Flap, so used stays below the boutique. You usually can't just buy a Birkin, two bags a year, by allocation, the wait. So the used market becomes the real market, and it sits above retail. Over the last decade the Birkin appreciated around 92%.
>
> So Chanel manufactures value with the price tag. Hermès does it with scarcity. Want a Flap? Used saves you money. Want a Birkin? Used is just the price of skipping the line.
