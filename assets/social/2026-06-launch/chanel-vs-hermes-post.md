## TikTok / Reel (open with a hook; it's a 60-90s explainer)
> Both of these "hold value." But here's the part nobody explains. A Chanel Classic Flap is about $11,700 new. Buy it gently used? Around $6,000. Half. The Birkin 30 is about $14,900 new. Gently used? Around $18,000. More than new.
>
> Same reputation, opposite math. And it's not because Chanel is cheap. Chanel actually raised its price faster, the Flap nearly doubled in five years. Hermès raised the Birkin slower.
>
> The difference is the door. You can walk into Chanel and buy a Flap, so resale stays below the boutique. You usually can't just buy a Birkin, there's a quota, two bags a year, the relationships, the wait. So the resale market becomes the real market, and it sits above retail. Over the last decade the Birkin appreciated around 92%.
>
> So Chanel manufactures value with the price tag. Hermès does it with scarcity. Want a Flap? Pre-owned saves you money. Want a Birkin? Pre-owned is just the price of skipping the line.
